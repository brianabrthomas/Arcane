Problem Statement

Corporate litigation creates billions of dollars of market risk, but the information needed to price that risk is fragmented across court dockets, SEC filings, legal briefs, news releases, and expert legal interpretation. Lawyers understand arguments, procedural posture, and precedent, while investors care about probabilities, timelines, damages exposure, and stock-price impact. Today, there is no liquid, transparent, real-time market layer that converts public corporate legal proceedings into forecastable probabilities. This leaves hedge funds, public-market investors, legal analysts, and companies relying on slow research workflows, subjective opinions, and expensive legal analytics platforms that do not produce tradable consensus probabilities. 

Product Description 
Arcane is an agentic prediction market platform for public corporate litigation. Autonomous agents discover public lawsuits, summarize docket activity, extract market-moving legal catalysts, identify comparable precedent, estimate legal-risk probabilities, and trade paper or testnet prediction contracts using USDC nanopayments. The platform turns legal events into binary markets, such as whether a company settles before trial, whether an injunction is granted, or whether a motion is denied by a specific date. An automated market maker prices YES/NO contracts in real time, while a nanopayment economy allows agents to get paid for docket summaries, legal-risk scores, case analytics, liquidity quotes, and resolution services. The prediction market becomes the final pricing layer for an agentic legal-intelligence economy. 
Value Propositions 

Lawyers assess cases using subjective arguments, but market makers and hedge funds only care about probabilities and timelines. A prediction market strips away legal jargon and turns qualitative legal proceedings into quantitative, tradable assets. For investors, the platform converts complex litigation into tradable probabilities and potential hedging instruments tied to public-company outcomes. For lawyers and legal analysts, it creates a way to monetize expertise by contributing structured legal judgments that improve market probability pricing. For prediction market users, it provides markets rooted in real-world corporate events with objective resolution criteria.
